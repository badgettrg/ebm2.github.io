Cited reference searches with Scholar

  • The Scholar button can expedite cited reference searching. Cited reference searching is an alternative to traditional (Boolean) searching. The premise is that if you have a seminal article for a topic, any high-quality subsequent article will cite the seminal article. Example:
    • A collection of 5 existing articles on sepsis alerts that you may have gathered from UpToDate, Dynamed, colleagues, initial lit search, etc.
    • Which of these is the most highly cited?
    • Then ask Google to give you the list of articles that are published later and cite this article.
