2. Search strategy

General medical questions

Use something similar to the 6S model. Note the sequence:
  • Summaries such as Clinical Evidence, Dynamed, UpToDate (these may require subscriptions)
  • Systematic reviews (and practice guidelines if they are based on systematic reviews, eg. USPSTF)
  • Original studies from MEDLINE, via PubMed or other provider. Details of Medline searching
The basic concept of this model, that MEDLINE should be last, has been validated by https://pubmed.gov/17082828 which concluded this strategy is not more thorough, but seems faster.

Drug questions

  • Drug interactions. Use the interaction checker just below the search box on UpToDate's new search page
  • P450 interactions. Use the tables at Indiana's Centers for Education & Research on Therapeutics (CERTs)
    • Example: which beta-blocker should you avoid in patients on methadone?
